Safest Place to Raise a Family in Middle Earth

The Shire is honored to receive the prestigious "Safest Place to Raise a Family in Middle-earth" award, a testament to its tranquil and nurturing environment. With its low crime rates, tight-knit communities, and picturesque landscapes, The Shire offers families a peaceful haven where children can roam freely and neighbors support each other. This recognition underscores The Shire's commitment to providing a safe and welcoming home for all, making it an ideal destination for families seeking security and serenity.

The Mithril Goblet Award for Culinary Excellence

Celebrated as a hallmark of culinary mastery, this award recognizes the Green Dragon Inn's outstanding culinary creations, from hearty hobbit fare to delectable delicacies, delighting the palates of travelers and locals alike.

Median Age

The median age in The Shire is approximately 35 years old, reflecting a population that values longevity and a balanced lifestyle amidst the tranquility of their homeland.

Employment Rate

The employment rate in The Shire stands at an impressive 95%, with the majority of residents engaged in various occupations ranging from farming and gardening to crafts, hospitality, and administrative roles.

Healthcare

Healthcare in The Shire is exemplary, with a network of well-equipped healers' houses and herb gardens providing essential medical care to residents. The average life expectancy in The Shire exceeds 100 years, thanks to the expertise of healers, the abundance of wholesome food, and the peaceful lifestyle embraced by its inhabitants.

Transportation

Despite its predominantly rural character, The Shire boasts a well-maintained network of footpaths, dirt roads, and bridges connecting its picturesque villages and settlements. Pony-drawn carts and wagons are commonly used for transportation, while the occasional barge navigates the tranquil waters of the Brandywine River.

Features

Bag End

The ancestral home of the Baggins family, located atop the Hill in Hobbiton, renowned for its distinctive round door and beautiful gardens.

The Party Tree

At the heart of Hobbiton, the majestic Party Tree serves as a gathering spot for celebrations, adorned with lanterns and ribbons, fostering joy and camaraderie among hobbits.

The Brandywine Bridge

Spanning the Brandywine River, this historic stone bridge connects the Shire with Buckland, symbolizing unity and facilitating trade and travel across Middle-earth.

The Tuckborough Market

Bustling with vendors selling fresh produce and crafts, Tuckborough Market showcases the Shire's creativity and entrepreneurial spirit in a vibrant atmosphere of commerce and community.

The Far Downs

Offering panoramic views of the countryside, the Far Downs' rolling hills are a haven for adventurers seeking solitude amidst nature's beauty and wonder.

The Old Forest

Bordering Buckland, the Old Forest is a mysterious woodland steeped in legend, where ancient trees and winding paths beckon brave souls to explore its secrets and unravel its mysteries.
